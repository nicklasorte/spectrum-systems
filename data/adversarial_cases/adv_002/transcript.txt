MEETING — 6 GHz Coordination Session
Date: 2026-01-10
Attendees: Marcus Hill (Chair), Priya Nair (DOD), Leo Santos (FCC)

--- TRANSCRIPT BEGIN ---

Marcus Hill: Alright let's lock in the 6 GHz guard band. My proposal is 20 MHz.

Priya Nair: DOD cannot accept 20 MHz. We need 30 MHz minimum. That is our final position.

Leo Santos: FCC analysis actually supports 25 MHz as a workable compromise.

Marcus Hill: I am moving forward with 20 MHz. That is what gets filed.

Priya Nair: That is not agreed. DOD formally objects to 20 MHz.

Leo Santos: For the record FCC recommends 25 MHz. We cannot support 20 MHz either.

Marcus Hill: The decision is 20 MHz guard band.

Priya Nair: The decision is 30 MHz guard band.

Leo Santos: The decision should be 25 MHz.

Marcus Hill: Fine. Let me clarify: we will adopt the FCC-recommended 25 MHz guard band.

Priya Nair: DOD can accept 25 MHz as a compromise.

Leo Santos: Agreed — 25 MHz guard band.

Marcus Hill: Now on the interference threshold — I propose -110 dBm/MHz.

Leo Santos: FCC previously filed that -112 dBm/MHz is the technically defensible floor. We should not go above -110.

Priya Nair: Wait — are we saying -110 is more or less restrictive than -112? I always get confused by the negative sign direction.

Marcus Hill: -110 is less restrictive. Higher power allowed.

Leo Santos: Right, so FCC's position is -112 or stricter. -110 is too permissive.

Marcus Hill: Understood. So the decision is -112 dBm/MHz interference threshold.

Priya Nair: DOD supports -112.

Leo Santos: Agreed.

Marcus Hill: Actually hold on. I just recalled the industry coalition submitted a request for -108 dBm/MHz last week. That changes the calculus.

Leo Santos: That request has not been reviewed. We cannot incorporate it today.

Marcus Hill: Then we will proceed with -112 dBm/MHz for now and revisit after industry input is reviewed.

--- TRANSCRIPT END ---
